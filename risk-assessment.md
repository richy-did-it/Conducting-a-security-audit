# Botium Toys: Risk Assessment Report

This report summarizes the risk assessment conducted as part of the security audit for Botium Toys. It evaluates current controls, compliance, and potential risks to the organization.

---

## Risk Description

- Inadequate management of assets  
- Missing controls across IT systems  
- Potential non-compliance with U.S. and international regulations and standards

---

## Control Best Practices

- **Identify Function (NIST CSF):** Dedicate resources to identify and classify assets  
- **Impact Analysis:** Determine the potential business impact of asset loss, including critical systems  
- **Controls Implementation:** Implement recommended controls to reduce risk and improve security posture

---

## Risk Score

- **Score:** 8/10 (High risk)  
- **Reason:** Lack of controls and non-adherence to compliance best practices

---

## Additional Comments

The potential impact from the loss of assets is rated **medium**, as the IT department lacks knowledge of which assets are at risk. The risk of regulatory fines is **high**, due to missing controls and incomplete adherence to compliance best practices.

### Specific Findings:

- All employees currently have access to internally stored data, including cardholder and customersâ€™ PII/SPII  
- Encryption is not used for customer credit card data stored internally  
- No access controls enforcing **least privilege** or **separation of duties**  
- Availability and data integrity are ensured through integrated controls  
- Firewall blocks traffic based on defined security rules  
- Antivirus software is installed and monitored regularly  
- No Intrusion Detection System (IDS) is in place  
- No disaster recovery plans or backups for critical data  
- Plan exists to notify E.U. customers within 72 hours of a data breach  
- Privacy policies, procedures, and processes exist and are enforced among IT staff and employees  
- Existing password policy is minimal and does not meet modern complexity standards  
- No centralized password management system  
- Legacy systems are monitored but lack a regular schedule or clear intervention methods  
- Physical security: offices, storefront, and warehouse have locks, functioning CCTV, and fire detection/prevention systems
